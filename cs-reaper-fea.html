Engineering Project 2024

Design & FEA of Agricultural Reaper

Structural analysis and optimization of an agricultural reaper machine to address harvesting labor shortages and improve crop cutting efficiency.

Objective

Design Challenge

The increasing demand for manual workers during the harvesting season presents a significant challenge in modern agriculture. The objective was to design a robust agricultural reaper to efficiently cut crops like wheat and rice, mechanizing the process to reduce labor while maintaining structural reliability under varied field conditions.

Goal

  • Model a complete reaper assembly including the main frame, cutting mechanism, and conveying system.
  • Perform static structural and fatigue analysis to evaluate equivalent (von-Mises) stress and total deformation.
  • Compare materials (such as AISI 1095 High Carbon Steel and IS2062 Mild Steel) to ensure a balance between strength and operational efficiency.

Process & Tech Stack

01 Assembly Design

Engineered a comprehensive 3D CAD assembly integrating the main frame, fixed and reciprocating cutter bars, star wheels, and power transmission system.

02 Meshing & Loading

Executed fine-tuned meshing and applied realistic boundary conditions, including a 2000N perpendicular cutting force on the blades and 3000N horizontal motion resistance on the frame.

The static structural analysis validated the mechanical integrity of the reaper design under simulated field loads. Utilizing AISI 1095 High Carbon Steel for the cutter and IS2062 Mild Steel for the main frame ensured lower deformation and high stability. The insights gained from the stress distribution and fatigue analysis facilitated design refinements, ensuring the machinery can withstand continuous cutting stresses while maximizing harvesting productivity.
